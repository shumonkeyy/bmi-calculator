FAQs

BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a numerical value derived from an individualâ€™s weight and height. It is commonly used as a screening tool to categorize people into different weight status categories and assess whether they are underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.

While BMI is widely used, it does not measure body fat directly and may not accurately represent health for certain individuals, such as athletes with high muscle mass or older adults with low muscle mass. It should be interpreted alongside other health indicators.

BMI might not be accurate for:
  • Athletes or individuals with high muscle mass (it may categorize them as overweight).
  • Older adults with lower muscle mass.
  • Pregnant individuals.
  • Children and teens (special growth charts are used for them).
